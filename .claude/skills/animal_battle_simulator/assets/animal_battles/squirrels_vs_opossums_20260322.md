### Battle Narrative

The oak woodland goes quiet just before it begins.

The 62 squirrels occupy the canopy and mid-story branches, chattering furiously, tails twitching like war flags. Below, 77 opossums waddle out of the underbrush — a grey, hissing wall of low-slung fury, pink noses raised and 50-toothed maws agape.

The squirrels make the first move. They rain down a barrage of acorns, pine cones, and sheer aggressive chatter from above. Several opossums take direct acorn hits to the snout and recoil, hissing. But opossums are not easily rattled. They begin climbing.

This is where the battle turns interesting.

The opossums are slow climbers, and the squirrels use this ruthlessly — spiraling up and down trunks with dazzling speed, slashing with tiny claws, and vanishing before a single opossum jaw can close. The 3D battlefield should be a squirrel paradise.

But the opossums have a trick: three of them suddenly go limp and "play dead," flopping from low branches directly onto unsuspecting squirrels below, crushing them under a wave of dead weight and immediately biting. The squirrel ranks break in confusion — how do you fight an enemy that just becomes a lump?

The numbers begin to tell. 77 opossums occupy every trunk, every low branch. The squirrels are corralled upward into the thinner canopy where their acorn ammunition runs thin. Squeezed from below by a relentless grey tide, the tribe of 62 squirrels fractures — half scatter into neighboring trees and flee, the other half make a desperate last stand, leaping from opossum back to opossum back like furry pinballs.

It is chaotic, impressive, and ultimately futile.

The opossums, slow but inexorable, consolidate control of the woodland floor and the lower canopy. The remaining squirrels retreat to the very treetops, exhausted and out of acorns.
